Bed time stories

The Honest Woodcutter

Once upon a time, there lived a poor woodcutter in a small village surrounded by a dense forest. Every morning, he woke up before sunrise and walked into the forest carrying his old axe. He worked hard throughout the day, cutting fallen branches and gathering firewood to sell in the nearby market. The money he earned was just enough to support his family, but he never complained about his difficult life. One afternoon, while chopping a tree near a river, his axe slipped from his hands and fell into the deep water. The woodcutter searched desperately, but the river was too deep. He sat on the riverbank and began to cry. Suddenly, a magical fairy appeared and asked him why he was sad. After hearing his story, she dived into the river and returned with a golden axe. The woodcutter honestly said it was not his. She then brought a silver axe, but again he refused it. Finally, she returned with his old iron axe. The fairy was pleased with his honesty and rewarded him with all three axes. The woodcutter thanked her and returned home happily. His honesty became famous throughout the village, and everyone admired his character and integrity for many years.

Moral Lesson

Honesty is always rewarded. Speaking the truth and maintaining integrity, even in difficult situations, earns respect, trust, and unexpected rewards.

The Lion and the Mouse

One warm afternoon in a large jungle, a powerful lion was sleeping peacefully beneath a shady tree. While he rested, a tiny mouse was running around searching for food. Without noticing the lion, the mouse accidentally ran across his huge paw. The lion immediately woke up and trapped the frightened mouse beneath his claws. The mouse trembled with fear and begged the lion to spare his life. He promised that one day he would return the favor. The lion laughed loudly because he could not imagine such a small creature helping the king of the jungle. Nevertheless, he felt kind and released the mouse. Several days later, hunters entered the jungle and captured the lion in a strong rope net. The lion struggled fiercely, but he could not escape. His loud roars echoed through the forest. Hearing the familiar roar, the little mouse rushed to help. Using his sharp teeth, he began chewing through the ropes. After working tirelessly, he finally cut enough ropes for the lion to break free. The grateful lion thanked the mouse and realized that size does not determine value. From that day onward, they became close friends. Their story taught all the animals that kindness, friendship, and gratitude can create strong bonds between even the most unlikely companions.

Moral Lesson

No one is too small to make a difference. Kindness shown to others often returns when it is needed most, regardless of size, status, or strength.

The Farmer and the Golden Corn

In a peaceful countryside village, there lived a hardworking farmer who owned a small field of corn. Every season, he carefully prepared the soil, planted seeds, watered the crops, and protected them from pests. Although farming required great effort, he enjoyed watching his plants grow taller each day. One year, he noticed a particular corn plant that looked healthier and stronger than all the others. Its leaves were greener, its stalk was taller, and its ears of corn shone brightly in the sunlight. When harvest season arrived, the farmer discovered that the plant had produced beautiful golden-colored corn unlike anything he had ever seen. Instead of keeping the seeds for himself, he decided to share them with neighboring farmers. The villagers planted the seeds and followed the farmer’s advice on proper cultivation. The next harvest was the best the village had ever experienced. Fields were full of healthy crops, and everyone enjoyed greater prosperity. People from nearby villages came to learn the secret of their success. The farmer explained that success grows when knowledge and resources are shared. His generosity inspired others to help one another whenever possible. Over time, the village became famous for both its rich harvests and its cooperative spirit, proving that kindness and teamwork can benefit an entire community.

Moral Lesson

Sharing knowledge and helping others leads to collective success. Cooperation, generosity, and teamwork create prosperity for everyone.
